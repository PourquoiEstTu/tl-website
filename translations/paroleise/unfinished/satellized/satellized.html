Translation Notes

This is probably the most ambiguous song of the small amount I've translated. Because of that, I felt the need to add in my best guesses of what the subjects and/or agents are to some of the lines that are only one word in Japanese.

The original songs for satellized are Love-colored Master Spark and The Witches' Ball, both Marisa's themes from different games. Inspired by the album cover for dialogue～Stella Theater～ by kimino-museum and other art I'd seen from different arranges of The Witches' Ball, I was originally going to translate this as Marisa "pursuing" Alice or Patchouli, but the general theme of this album is about Reimu and Marisa. Further, this song, unlike The Hakurei Miko Passes Through, does not contain any explicit references to anyone else. As a result, I kept with Marisa's point of view and translated the lines where pursuing something or someone is described broad.

That said, what exactly Marisa is pursuing is never spelled out. It seems that what she's chasing is related to the person she goes somewhere with in the fifth verse. Assuming that that person is Reimu, I expand on this thought in the following paragraphs.

In line 2.3, what is actually being recalled by Marisa is up to interpretation. Given the verses after this line, it could be that she's chasing after Reimu's shadow, i.e. trying to match her in some quality such as strength, incident resolving (a reference to The Hakurei Miko Passes Through perhaps), etc. If you were to instead look at verse 1, it may be that Marisa is chasing after something more concrete, like an item or book, a possibility informed mostly by line 1.5. I left out any specific guesses as to what the "item" being pursued was due to this. As a related aside, the path from line 2.1 could also be a metaphorical path rather than a literal one, but the dual interpretation is easy to convey with the simple translation.

Line 3.1 is translated as dashing up to the distant future, but what is actually being dashed up towards could also have been the hill in the next line. This would leave the 未来遠く as simply a statement on the future being distant. I went with the former because another verb is already attached to the 星の丘, though the meaning would fit if Marisa dashed up to the hill, then travelled up it.

Line 6.3 also had a lot of thought put into it because of how many meanings 向こう has. It could mean a/the destination on the hill, the other side (the decline opposite to the incline Marisa goes up) of the hill, and simply, somewhere beyond the hill. I went with the latter because I felt the first two assumed too much information I didn't have. Contrary to this sentiment of not wanting to assume too much, I assumed that the "it" Marisa is chasing in line 6.2 (which is maybe the same as the "it" she tries to recall in line 2.3) is located beyond the hill because the line says 丘のその向こう instead of just 丘の向こう. Of course, I could be reading into it too much, but the assumption ties up the verse neatly.

This wraps up my thoughts on my translation choices which I thought warranted an explanation. Now, I would like to give an alternate interpretation to the song. As can be seen from my commentary above, I assumed that what Marisa is pursuing is located in the real world, or at the least, within the normal bounds of her travel in Gensokyo, whether that be Reimu, or something else. Instead, Marisa may be chasing after someone who is deceased, that being Reimu of course. This interpretation is motivated by fanworks portraying Reimu as remaining a human throughout her life and Marisa eventually choosing to become a (youkai) Magician and 未来 being able to be translated as "the world beyond the grave". With such an interpretation, the first verse can be seen as Marisa currently being in the living world (note the mention of the summer and the exhilarating wind) and looking for clues on something. Perhaps that something may be on how to get into the afterlife while still living. This line of thought can be continued into the second verse, where Marisa is lost on a path she followed to the afterlife. Lines 2.3 and 2.4 could then be detailing how she's been lost for so long that she forgot what she came here for and even when she remembers it, she no longer cares. Climbing up the hill of stars could be a reference to going up to heaven, and maybe the path she was lost on was on that hill. The fifth verse then becomes about how Marisa reclaims her will to chase after Reimu in the afterlife and that she knows magic precisely for extraordinary times like this. Finally, the last verse is about her giving up on the chase and accepting Reimu's death. This verse may even signal that Marisa was still human and not a youkai during this whole ordeal. There were many leaps of logic along the way, but there are lines in the song pointing toward this, so maybe this interpretation is correct. Likely not though, and I did not translate the song with this interpretation.
