Translation Notes

I made the line breaks between verses myself since the lyrics don't have any. They're based on how the song flows so they shouldn't be too off?

The second verse/stanza was definitely the most difficult to translate. I felt like there were a lot of different ways to arrange the lines. It might have made sense to group the first two lines along with the fourth and have the third as the stand-alone since I treated it as the "conclusion" of the first half of the stanza. Specifically, it concluded Sakuya being alone and transitions into the second half of the stanza where Sakuya seemingly finds or is found by Remillia. I ended up grouping the first two lines together and then the fourth with first half of the third line. I'm fairly sure that the third line shouldn't be translated literally as "A town that stood still without any reason to live" since the rest of the stanza seems to talk about Sakuya before she started working at the Scarlet Devil Mansion. So, I used the "the town stood still" part to transition into the second half where Sakuya supposedly meets Remillia.

The rest of the second stanza was mostly straightforward except for "夜めて" in the 6th line. I couldn't find any word that matches up with online at all besides "夜目" which is a noun. If "in the dark" or "seeing in the dark" was the intending meaning, I feel like the "て"-form for nouns and not for ru-verbs would have been used. It was pronounced as "やめて" in the song, so I decided to interpret it as the flower petals move in the morning because of the cold morning wind, but then stop (fall asleep) when the morning wind stops at night. I think it reads well, but I'm not very sure if its correct translation.

Lastly, I wanted to mention the translation for the first line of the 4th stanza, "たとえ明日心移ろう声に惑い消されても". "心移ろう声" was a really difficult one. A definition I found for 心移ろう was "change the target of someone's love/affection to something/ someone else" or more more generally, "someone's feelings changing". I initially decided to translate this as "fickle" since I couldn't think of any word or phrase which meant the same and flowed well. "fickle" somewhat encapsulates the feeling of love and a kind of arbitrary-ness I wanted compared to a word like "capricious" which doesn't really have the nuance of love/affection. After messing around with a thesaurus for a bit, I ended up at "coquettish" which gives off the same kind of jumpy "arbitrary" feeling as "fickle" and also has the nuance of being flirtatious and teasing which I thought fit well as a description of a voice. The flirtatious nuance fit well with the translation I went with for 惑い. Of the possible translations, I thought "illusion" and "infatuation" fit the best. "Illusion" fit well since for this line in particular, it captures a feeling of overestimation and being overexcited about something which may not have been impressive or worth being excited about. In the context of the song, I felt like it could have meant Remillia got bored of Sakuya's time-stopping ability or of her perfection as a servant. The reason I went with "infatuation" over it was because of the connotation of love/affection coming from "心移ろう声". "Infatuation" can capture the same meanings "illusion" can but can also more explicitly carry meanings of love, usually intense love. For example, an obsessive, overwhelming love, or a love that disregards all faults in one's partner could be interpretations drawn from the word. I intend it to mean the same as what I wanted "illusion" to represent except with a more concrete connotation of love. Its a more specific word than "illusion", so it may be easier to come up with a different interpretation than mine while reading, but the same idea is conveyed so it's fine to me.
